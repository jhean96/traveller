Nacpan Beach

For an easy day trip out of El Nido, head north by tricycle or motorbike to this incredible, golden-hued, 3km-long beach. Development has arrived in Nacpan and new guesthouses, boutique hotels and upscale tented camps are popping up everywhere. Still, the atmosphere remains mellow – for now. To get here, drive 16km north of El Nido on the National Hwy (paved), then another 4km down a once-rough access road that is now mostly sealed. There's a shuttle van from El Nido.

Activities

Watch out for dropping jaws when you and your friends venture on the El Nido Island Hopping Hidden Beaches and Shrine Tour, which is all about mind-blowing rock formations, dreamy beaches,snorkling, and enchanting lagoons which can only be found in Bacuit Bay.

How To Get To Nacpan Beach?

With the Nacpan Beach Shuttle Service: For only P600 per person, you can now organise your round trip to Nacpan Beach with a aircon van and a free pick up and drop off to your hotel in El Nido or Corong-Corong. With a tricycle: The price for 2 persons is around 1000PHP. The trip will take you around 1 hour. Know that it will not be a confortable one since half of the road is in a very bad state. It can even get really muddy if it has been raining in the previous days.

Saud Beach

The jewel in the crown of Pagudpud is Saud Beach, with its renowned wonderful fine white sand. The waters of the South China Sea are a magnificent blue and crystal clear all the time.

How to get there?

Nearest Airport: Laoag International Airport (Ilocos Norte)

By Air

Philippine Airlines and Cebu Pacific Air have regular flights from Manila to Laoag International Airport. Either from your hotel or city proper, there are several tricycles waiting for tourists to take them to their resorts in Saud Beach.

By Land

For those who prefer traveling by land, Ilocos Norte and Sur are accessible via Metro Manila’s bus stations in Cubao and Manila. The bus fare from Cubao to Vigan (Ilocos Sur) costs approximately P665 to P805, while those traveling to Laoag costs around P800 to P965 all depending on the bus company and class of the seat. The popular bus companies for these routes are Florida Bus and Partas. Your gateway to Saud Beach is either Vigan or Laoag; if you arrive at the former, you’ll need to board another bus bound for Laoag, which costs P120 per way. Once at the terminal take a tricycle to another bus station bound for Pagudpud, which costs around P60. Alight at the Municipal Hall because that’s where several tricycles are waiting for tourists to take them to their resorts in Saud Beach.

Laiya Beach

Laiya has white sand beaches and clear waters, and as such is frequently compared to Boracay,which is the country's top beach choice for local tourists, celebrities, and foreign tourists alike. Weathering-formed crushed shells and corals make up both beaches' white sands, with Boracay's being noticeably finer and whiter. Laiya is one of the beaches that have gained popularity recently[when?]; thus, there are not as many luxury resorts and activities in the area as compared to other beaches in the country. It is within driving distance (from two to six hours depending on traffic) from the city of Manila.

Activities

Things to do in Laiya:

1.Swim. Laiya's crystal blue waters are safe, clear and undertow-free.

2.Ride a boat. Accredited operators provide boats that can be rented (for trips to marine sanctuaries, resort-hopping, etc.)

3.Go snorkeling or scuba diving.

4.Go kayaking.

How To Get To Laiya Beach?

Via San Juan

The fastest way to Laiya is via San Juan. Take ALPS Bus from either EDSA Nepa Q-Mart, Araneta Center Cubao, or Alabang Grand Terminal to San Juan, Batangas. The earliest trip leaves at 3:00AM and the last trip departs at 5:00PM. ALPS Bus has 12 total departures per day. The fare is ₱190.

In San Juan, right in front of the Municipal Hall, board a Laiya- or Hugom-bound jeepney. Take note that the last departing jeepney to Laiya leaves at 5:00PM. There are also tricycles that are willing to take you to Laiya but you just need to shell out more.

Via Lipa

From Pasay Buendia, EDSA Kamuning, or Araneta Center Cubao, take Ceres Tours, DLTB, JAM, Batangas Star Express, RRCG, etc to Lipa City, Batangas. Travel Time is about two hours and the fare ranges from ₱135 to ₱150.

From Lipa bus terminal, take a jeepney or van to San Juan, Batangas. The van is faster but will cost you much higher.

La Union Beach

La Union has become a favorite destination among weekend warriors from Manila; thanks to the swell in Urbiztondo that has turned San Juan into a resort town. Accessibility is another thing. Yes, San Juan is 5-7 hours away, but the journey is pretty straightforward. You just board a bus in Manila and you can alight right in Urbiztondo.

Activities

1.Surfing

2.visit La union Culture and Arts Gallery.

3.Trek to Tangadan

4.Drop by Honeybee Center

5.Go on Culinary Adventure

How To Get To La Union Beach?

Take a bus bound for La Union and alight at tell the conductor you are getting at Urbiztondo Beach in San Juan La Union. Bus fare to la Union from Manila is PHP 400. Travel time is 5 to 7 hours depending on traffic or where you are coming from.
